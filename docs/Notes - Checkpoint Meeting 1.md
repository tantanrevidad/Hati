## **Hackathon Overview**

- The APAC Hackathon 2026 is a one-month running event focused on Vietnam, Indonesia, and the Philippines.
- The primary goal is to build financial applications (mobile or web) catering to financial inclusion, cross-border payments, and emerging markets, specifically targeting the Southeast Asian (APAC) context.
- It is hosted by Stellar, with a focus on utilizing the Stellar network.

## **Submission Guidelines**

- **Idea Submission:** Required by July 10th to track active teams. If your idea changes, you do not need to resubmit; update it for the final submission to avoid being flagged as spam.
- **Final Project Submission (July 15th):**
- **GitHub Repository:** Must be public. Code should demonstrate the technical implementation.
- **Demo Video:** A link must be accessible and included in the README.
- **Pitch Deck:** Slides must be accessible and linked in the README.
- **Working MVP:** Deployed on the testnet (mainnet is encouraged, and funds can be provided if needed). Must have a functional frontend/UI.
- **README File:** Critical for judges. Must detail the project vision, features, problem solved, team members (names and optional photos/logos), and metrics for scalability.
- **Team Structure:** Maximum of 5 members. Solo developers are welcome and encouraged. Team changes can be made up until the final submission.

## **Evaluation Metrics (Demo Day)**

The July 18th Demo Day will evaluate projects based on:

1. **Technical Implementation and Usage:** The extent and quality of Stellar network integration.
2. **Real-World Fit and Use Case:** The practical application and problem-solving capability of the project.
3. **Innovation and Differentiation:** How the project stands out from existing solutions on Stellar or other blockchains.
4. **User Experience (UX) and Accessibility:** The simplicity and usability of the application during the demo.
5. **Go-to-Market Strategy:** Identifying the target audience (e.g., B2B, specific user profiles) and the project's scalability potential.
6. **Team:** The background and capabilities of the team members (e.g., past hackathon experience, existing projects). Highlighting team strengths in the README and pitch is crucial.

## **Tips for Success**

- **Global/APAC Context:** Ensure your solution is applicable beyond just the Philippines and addresses financial issues across the APAC region.
- **Stellar Integration:** Utilize Stellar tools heavily. The Freighter wallet is highly recommended over MetaMask. Consider using Stellar ecosystem features like SEP (Stellar Ecosystem Proposals), particularly SEP-24 (anchor integration), to strengthen your technical implementation score. Using the Stellar SDK for wallets is also advised for easier integration.
- **Detailed Documentation:** A thorough README is essential. Include all necessary links and team details. Creating an X (formerly Twitter) account for your project can also demonstrate scalability intent.
- **Testing:** Ensure your app is fully functional and free of major bugs before submission.
- **Mentorship:** Schedule mentoring sessions via the provided calendar link before it closes on July 15th.

## **Beyond the Hackathon**

- **Instawards:** Smaller grants ($5k - $15k USD) for builders to further develop their projects on Stellar. A great next step for hackathon projects showing promise. Active GitHub commits post-hackathon strengthen applications.
- **Stellar Community Fund (SCF):** Larger, pre-seed type grants for projects ready to scale as startups. Reviewing past SCF projects (currently up to SCF40) can provide inspiration and insight into what Stellar funds.
- **Feedback:** All participants will receive score sheets and feedback from the judges after the July 18th Demo Day to help improve their projects for future opportunities.

## **Q&A and Miscellaneous**

- **Prizes:** The total prize pool is $60,000, divided across categories (e.g., Local Finance, DeFi Ecosystem). Consolation prizes are significant (e.g., $3,000).
- **Presentation Format:** The July 18th Demo Day at the GCash office is hybrid. Teams from provinces (e.g., Davao, Iloilo, Bacolod) or those unable to attend in person can present online. The event will likely be held in the afternoon.
- **Licenses:** You can choose the license for your public repository (e.g., MIT). Stellar prefers public, open-source building.
- **User Metrics:** While not a strict judging criterion, having early users validates feasibility and strengthens the pitch.
